The Cause : Feature Projects

Our Projects in India

Association for India's Development works with over 100 projects in over 22 states in India in areas such as education, livelihoods, environemtn, agriculture, health, women's empowerment and social justice.

We work with non profits in India that work closely with the community to ensure participation and a voice in their own empowerment. We review projects and all projects are visited by AID volunteers to learn about first hand realities on the ground.

Here are some projects that we wanted to highlight to showcase our parters and the work they are doing on the ground.

Gallery : Feature Projects

Sustainable Agriculture in Sunderbans

Cyclone Aila had devasted the delta area of Sunderbans in 2008 by salinating the land and making the land useless. AID's intervension with several partner organizations started work with 25 farmers at that time and now has expanded to over 10,000 farmers.

Meaningful Youth Education

Our partner, Swanirbar works with over 11000 youths in 50 villages to train them in issues and skills for their immediate surroundings in West Bengal.
They have won awards for their civil participation from the state.


Payvihir Eco Village

AID partnered with local NGO, Khoj to work with local tribal communities who planted 50,000 trees along with other environmental and livelihood initiatives with NREGA.
They won the UNDP biodiversity award in 2014.


RTI on wheels

RTI on wheels has reached 80,000 people in Gujarat, Rajasthan, Maharastra, J&K, & Goa. It has answered 150,000 calls on filing Right to Information applications and taking civic ownership over community development and continues its journey across India.

Save the Girl Child

Amhi Amchya Arogysakhi's Save the Girl Child program works with 9 slums for the healthcare of over 50000 urban slum population raising awareness on women's health issues and has been key to reducing female foeticide in the local population.
